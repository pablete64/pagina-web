Research

Development of a hyperspectral image-guided robotic surgery system.

Publication date: 2024

This Bachelor Thesis will design the setup as well as the software development to mobilise a robot towards a specific contour guided by hyperspectral images. To carry out this task, the UR5e robotic system from Universal Robots has been chosen. Advanced technologies such as hyperspectral imaging and remote sensing have been integrated, with an adjustment to the context of robotic surgery. The importance of collaborative robotics and the application of hyperspectral imaging in various fields are discussed theoretically at the beginning of the study. It was decided that the URse robot would be used in this situation because of its strong structural qualities relevant to the topic at hand, such as its payload capacity, precision and adaptability. The robot's compatibility with the ROS operating system and its potential for system and device integration are also highlighted. Due to its excellent compatibility, the combination of ROS and Python forms the basis of this project when creating the software. It takes care of the robotic arm movements, the camera integration and the virtualisation of the environment. The Moveit! and Gazebo addons have been used to complete this task, in addition to the application of the transformations, all with the aim of automating the calculation of the trajectories and poses that the robot must perform within the virtual world. Next, the process of calibrating the camera, which directs the robotic arm towards the intended targets and converts the pixels into Cartesian coordinates for the robot's task map, is analysed. This is done by using OpenCV and other remote sensing software frameworks. Ultimately, the study concludes that all necessary components could be integrated into the investigated system. This demonstrates that the technology not only increases the accuracy and safety of surgical procedures, but also creates new opportunities for new studies and uses in the area of robotic surgery.
